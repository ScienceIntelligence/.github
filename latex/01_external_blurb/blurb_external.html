OpenScientists
An open platform for AI-assisted scientific discovery
Project Brief  ·  April 2026  ·  Non-confidential
Ship a working technique directly to the people who use it. Skip the paper-shaped detour.

The problem

The path from a working technique to the users who benefit from it is too long.

  • A researcher who discovers something that works cannot simply share it. They must package it as a paper, hide the simplest piece in a larger narrative, cherry-pick results, and wait on committees.
  • With a paper in hand, they approach investors — another round of story-crafting, another set of gatekeepers.
  • Customers then demand still more papers before adopting the technique in practice.

Each step was useful once. In the current era of capable AI systems, most add latency rather than value.

Our answer

A point-to-point, public-interest platform where the unit of exchange is not a paper but a skill — a reusable, composable agent workflow that performs a concrete scientific task.

Contributed directly. Evaluated openly. Used immediately. Attributed transparently.

Sits inside a larger open agent ecosystem a general-purpose agent framework, an auto-research framework on top, a scientific skill benchmark, plus harness, memory, agentic RL, and reliable-agent modules — each open source, each with a named maintainer.

What we will do

  • Build and operate the open skill platform: share, discover, evaluate, exchange, compose.
  • Run empirical studies of AI-mediated contribution in peer review and on public software infrastructure (agent-proposed and agent-reviewed PRs).
  • Release open-source modules for the supporting stack.
  • Publish a methodology article with senior academic collaborators.
  • Maintain and iterate as the primitives of AI-assisted work evolve.

How contributors are credited

Every contribution is attributed on the non-profit's homepage, technical reports, and platform pages — tokens donated, frameworks contributed, benchmark tasks authored, skills released, compute supplied, studies co-designed. Where for-profit partners generate revenue through the platform, those revenues can be routed back as donations and redistributed to skill-contributing developers.

Why now
The defaults for AI-assisted science are being set now. Workflows, benchmarks, and interfaces standardized over the next one to two years will be difficult to displace. A credible open alternative has a short window in which to be established — and OpenScientists exists to use it.
